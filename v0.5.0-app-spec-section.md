## 2. Rule 5 Draft

### Overview
Each offseason, after the annual draft, teams may select unprotected minor leaguers from other organizations. Players selected must be kept on the 25-man roster for the entire following season or offered back to their original team for $50K.

### Protection Rules
- Players on the 40-man roster are protected
- Players NOT on 40-man after 4 years in system (ages 18-20 when signed) or 3 years (ages 21+) are eligible
- Each team may protect players by adding them to 40-man (up to 40 total)

### Draft Structure
- Runs in reverse standings order (same as annual draft)
- Each team may select 0-3 players
- Selecting team pays $100K to original team (stored in transactions)
- Selected player: must stay on 25-man all season, or be offered back

### GM Behavior
- Analytics GM: aggressively protects top prospects, poaches undervalued relievers and corner bats
- Old-school GM: protects veterans, slower to add prospects to 40-man (sometimes leaves them exposed)
- Small market GMs: exploit Rule 5 to add MLB-ready players for cheap

### Return Mechanism
- If selected player can't stick on 25-man (injury, performance): offered back at game 30 check
- Original team may accept return (player goes back to 40-man) or decline (player becomes free agent)

### API
- `GET /api/draft/rule5` — current Rule 5 eligible players by team
- `POST /api/draft/rule5/protect` — add player to 40-man (GM action, procedural)

## 3. International Amateur Signing

### Overview
A separate signing period each offseason where teams sign 16-17 year old international prospects. Each team has an international bonus pool determined by market size and recent draft spending. Prospects are scouted and rated — but ratings are hidden until signing (scouts have varying accuracy).

### Bonus Pools
```
Mega market:   $8-12M pool
Large market:  $5-8M pool
Medium market: $3-5M pool
Small market:  $1.5-3M pool
```
Pool resets each offseason. Unspent pool does not carry over.

### Prospect Generation
- 30-50 international prospects generated each offseason
- Ages 16-17
- Origins: Dominican Republic (~35%), Venezuela (~25%), Cuba (~15%), Japan (~10%), South Korea (~5%), other (~10%)
- Each prospect has a true rating (hidden) and a scouted rating (scout accuracy ± 10-20 points)
- Potential A/B/C/D — also hidden, revealed at age 25 as normal

### Scout Accuracy
- Each team has a scouting_rating (1-10, derived from budget + GM archetype)
- Analytics GM: scouting_rating +2 for international (data-driven)
- Old-school GM: scouting_rating -1 for international (traditional bias)
- High scouting_rating: ±5-10 point accuracy on true rating
- Low scouting_rating: ±15-20 point accuracy

### Signing Logic (procedural)
- GMs bid based on scouted rating × position scarcity × potential
- Analytics GMs target high-upside signings (potential A/B weighted heavily)
- Old-school GMs target current rating
- Mega market teams outbid for top prospects
- Small market teams target undervalued prospects other teams miss

### Signed players
- Assigned to Rookie ball
- Service time clock does NOT start until first MLB appearance
- Must be on 40-man by age 19 or become free agent

### New DB fields
- players: is_international_signee (bool), signing_bonus (integer)
- teams: international_bonus_pool, scouting_rating

### API
- `GET /api/international/prospects` — current international prospect pool
- `GET /api/international/signings` — this offseason's completed signings

## 4. Player Opt-Outs and Arbitration

### Arbitration
Players with 3-6 years of service time who are not free agents enter arbitration if their team does not offer a contract by the deadline.

**Salary determination (procedural):**
```
arb_salary = market_value × arb_year_modifier
  arb_year_1 (3 yrs service): 40% of market value
  arb_year_2 (4 yrs service): 60% of market value
  arb_year_3 (5 yrs service): 80% of market value

market_value = overall_rating × position_multiplier × age_modifier
```

**Team decisions:**
- Non-tender: team declines to offer contract → player becomes free agent
  - Analytics GMs non-tender freely when projected arb salary > value
  - Old-school GMs reluctant to non-tender veterans
- Accept arb: team pays arb salary, player signs 1-year deal
- Multi-year offer: team offers 2-3 year deal at slight discount to avoid future arb

### Opt-Outs
Stars (overall 80+) signing multi-year contracts may negotiate an opt-out clause:
- Opt-out after year 2 or 3 of a long-term deal
- Player exercises opt-out if: current market value > remaining contract value by 20%+
- Leadership >= 70: less likely to opt out (loyalty factor)
- Analytics GM: always includes opt-out language for big signings (flexibility)
- Opt-out exercise: player re-enters free agency, massive news event

### New DB fields
- players: arb_year (1/2/3/null), has_opt_out (bool), opt_out_after_year (int), opted_out (bool)

### API
- `GET /api/arbitration/eligible` — players eligible for arbitration this offseason

## 5. Rivalries

### Overview
When two teams meet repeatedly in high-stakes situations, a rivalry forms. Tracked narratively, surfaces in news and timeline.

### Rivalry Formation
A rivalry forms between two teams when:
- They meet in the playoffs 2+ times within 5 seasons, OR
- One team blocks another from a division title 3+ times, OR
- A major trade between two teams goes wrong for one side (generated rivalry)

### Rivalry Intensity
```
rivalry_score (0-100):
  +20 per playoff series matchup
  +10 per division title denied
  +15 per controversial trade
  -5 per season without meaningful interaction
  cap: 100
```

### Rivalry Effects
- When rivals meet in regular season: attendance +15% (heated games)
- Rivalry matchup in playoffs: LLM generates special narrative, not just standard recap
- News feed highlights rival matchups with RIVALRY badge
- Watch tab: crowd fill animation more intense during rivalry games

### Timeline Surface
- Each season recap includes "Rivalry Watch" section if any rivalry score > 50
- Championship between rivals: special above-the-fold treatment

### New DB table: rivalries
- id, team_a_id, team_b_id, rivalry_score, formed_season, last_updated_season, origin_type

### API
- `GET /api/rivalries` — all active rivalries with scores

## 6. Award Races

### Overview
MVP, Cy Young, and Rookie of the Year tracked live during the season. Late-season drama as leaders change. Award voting fires at season end.

### Tracked Awards
- **MVP** (one per league, so 2 total): best position player, weighted: OPS (40%), RBI (20%), HR (20%), team wins (20%)
- **Cy Young** (one per league): best pitcher, weighted: ERA (35%), wins (20%), SO (25%), WHIP (20%)
- **Rookie of the Year** (one per league): players with < 1 year service time, same stats as MVP

### Live Tracking
- Updated every 5 games
- Displayed in League tab: "Award Races" panel showing current leader + nearest challenger
- Watch tab news ticker highlights when lead changes hands
- News item generated when lead changes: "[Player] takes AL MVP lead from [Player]"

### Voting (offseason, procedural)
- 30 voters (one per franchise)
- Old-school voters: favor counting stats (HR, RBI, wins)
- Analytics voters: favor efficiency (OPS, ERA, WHIP)
- Winner announced as a news event with LLM flavor line

## 7. Records Chasing

### Overview
When a player approaches a significant career milestone, the sim generates ongoing news coverage. Fans and the league pay attention.

### Tracked Milestones
Career hitting:
- 500 HR (first alert at 490), 600 HR (alert at 585), 700 HR (alert at 680)
- 3000 hits (alert at 2950)
- .400 season AVG (alert at .380 with 30+ games remaining)

Career pitching:
- 300 wins (alert at 290)
- 3000 K (alert at 2950)
- 1.00 career ERA over 10+ seasons (alert at 1.10)

Single-season:
- 60 HR in a season (alert at 50 HR with 20+ games remaining)
- 25 wins in a season (alert at 20 wins with 15+ games remaining)

### News Coverage
- Alert fires: "Record Watch — [Player] needs X [stat] to reach [milestone]"
- Every 2 games after alert: brief update in news feed
- Achievement: pinned news item for 5 games, LLM generates special narrative
- Timeline: milestone achievements noted in that season's newspaper

## 8. Platoon Splits

### Overview
Left-handed and right-handed batters perform differently against same-handed vs opposite-handed pitchers. Smart GMs exploit this.

### Split Ratings
Each hitter gets two hidden modifiers generated at world gen:
- `vs_lefty_modifier` (-10 to +10)
- `vs_righty_modifier` (-10 to +10)

Each pitcher similarly:
- `vs_lefty_modifier` (-10 to +10)
- `vs_righty_modifier` (-10 to +10)

Overall modifier applied to win probability calculation for that matchup.

### GM Behavior
- Analytics GM: tracks splits, platoons low-vs-lefty hitters against lefty starters
- Old-school GM: ignores splits, plays best overall player regardless
- Platooning: two players share a position (e.g. LF), each starting against opposite-handed pitchers

### No UI change needed
Split modifiers are internal to the sim. No new UI elements — just affects win probability math and GM roster construction logic.

### New DB fields
- players: vs_lefty_modifier (integer -10 to +10), vs_righty_modifier (integer -10 to +10)

## 9. Bullpen Management

### Bullpen Roles
Each relief pitcher is assigned a role based on overall rating + composure + control:

| Role | Assignment Criteria | Usage Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Closer | Best reliever overall 70+, composure >= 65 | 9th inning, save situations only |
| Setup | Second-best reliever, overall 65+ | 7th-8th inning, high-leverage |
| Left Specialist | Best LOOGY (left-handed, vs_lefty_modifier >= 5) | Specific matchup only |
| Middle Relief | Remaining relievers overall 55-64 | 5th-7th inning |
| Long Relief | Lowest overall relievers, stamina >= 60 | Blowouts, SP emergencies |

### Usage Logic
- Closer: max 1 inning per game, needs 1 day rest after use
- Setup: max 1.1 innings, needs 1 day rest after use
- Overuse penalty: if used 3 days in a row, effectiveness -10% next appearance
- Analytics GM: strict role adherence, monitors usage carefully
- Old-school GM: uses best relievers regardless of role/rest

### Pitcher Fatigue (season-level)
- Relievers track appearances per season
- After 60 appearances: effectiveness -5%
- After 70 appearances: -10% + injury risk +5%
- Closer limited to 50 appearances max (analytics GM) or no limit (old-school)

### New DB fields
- players: bullpen_role (closer/setup/specialist/middle/long/null for non-relievers), appearances_this_season (int), consecutive_days_used (int)

## 10. Hot and Cold Streaks

### Overview
Players go on hot and cold streaks that temporarily affect their performance. Generates news items, makes late-season races more dramatic.

### Streak Mechanics
- Every 5 games: each MLB player has a 10% chance of entering a hot streak, 10% cold streak
- Hot streak: all offensive/pitching ratings +8 for 5-10 games
- Cold streak: all ratings -8 for 5-10 games
- Streaks cannot stack — a player on a hot streak cannot start a cold streak until hot streak ends
- Streak duration: random 5-10 games

### News Coverage
- Hot streak starting (5+ game hit streak or 3-start ERA < 1.50): news item generated
- Cold streak on a star (overall 80+): news item generated
- Streak ending for a notable player: brief news item

### Visible in UI
- Player card: streak indicator badge (🔥 hot / 🧊 cold)
- Standings: team on 5+ game winning streak shows streak indicator

### New DB fields
- players: streak_type (hot/cold/null), streak_games_remaining (int)
- teams: winning_streak (int), losing_streak (int) — already implied but make explicit

## 11. Stadium Upgrades

### Overview
Teams can invest in stadium upgrades that increase capacity, attendance, and revenue. Owners propose upgrades; analytics GMs evaluate ROI; old-school GMs push for prestige projects.

### Upgrade Types
| Upgrade | Cost | Effect | Build Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Premium seating expansion | $20M | Capacity +2,000, revenue +$5M/season | 1 offseason |
| New scoreboard | $10M | Attendance rate +3% (fan experience) | 1 offseason |
| Expanded concessions | $8M | Revenue +$3M/season | 1 offseason |
| New stadium (small→medium) | $150M | Capacity ×1.5, revenue +$20M/season | 3 seasons |
| New stadium (medium→large) | $300M | Capacity ×1.4, revenue +$40M/season | 3 seasons |

### Upgrade Decision Logic
- Owner must approve (net worth tier gates maximum spend)
- Analytics GM: approves upgrades with ROI < 5 seasons
- Old-school GM: approves upgrades that increase prestige regardless of ROI
- Meddling win-now owner: prefers spending on payroll over stadium
- Patient owner: more willing to invest in long-term infrastructure

### Relocation Context
Stadium deal (from relocation threat resolution) is a forced stadium upgrade — counts as New Stadium at city's expense (team cost = $0).

### New DB fields
- teams: stadium_upgrade_in_progress (bool), stadium_upgrade_complete_season (int), stadium_upgrade_type (varchar)

### API
- `GET /api/teams/:id/stadium` — current stadium details and upgrade history

## 12. Revenue Model Depth

### Full Revenue Formula (expanded from v0.4.0)
```
annual_revenue =
  base_market_revenue                          # market size tier
  + attendance_revenue                         # tickets × avg_ticket_price
  + premium_seating_revenue                    # luxury boxes, club seats
  + concessions_revenue                        # scales with attendance
  + broadcasting_revenue                       # flat per team, +bonus for playoff games
  + merchandise_revenue                        # scales with winning % and star players
  + playoff_revenue                            # significant bonus per round
  + championship_revenue                       # massive spike
  - luxury_tax_payments
  - stadium_debt_service                       # if new stadium financed
```

### Attendance Model
```
attendance_rate =
  base_attendance_rate (from market)
  × winning_modifier                           # above .500 = +modifier, below = -modifier
  × rivalry_game_modifier                      # +15% for rivalry matchups
  × playoff_race_modifier                      # +10% when within 5 games of playoff spot
  × weather_modifier                           # minor seasonal variation
  × new_stadium_honeymoon                      # +20% first 2 seasons after new stadium
  + star_player_bonus                          # team has overall 85+ player: +5% attendance
```

### Broadcasting Revenue
- Base: $25M per team per season (national deal split equally)
- Playoff bonus: +$5M per round advanced
- Championship: +$25M

### Merchandise Revenue
- Base: 2% of attendance_revenue
- Star player bonus: +$2M if team has overall 85+ player (jersey sales)
- Championship year: +$8M

## Known Edge Cases

- **Rule 5 player returned to original team:** if original team is now over 40-man limit (trades happened), player becomes free agent instead
- **Opt-out fires during season:** not allowed — opt-outs only exercised in offseason window
- **Rivalry between owned franchise and relocated team:** rivalry score carries over, original_city referenced in narrative
- **Award race leader retires mid-season:** award not given to retired player — second place wins
- **International prospect signs with team over pool limit:** not allowed — procedural logic caps spending at pool amount
- **Stadium upgrade in progress when relocation threat fires:** upgrade cancelled, costs forfeited if team relocates
- **Closer used 3 days in a row in turbo:** fatigue penalties still apply in turbo — never skipped
- **Hot streak player gets injured:** streak ends immediately on IL placement
- **Two players chasing same milestone simultaneously:** both tracked, both get news coverage, race narrative generated by LLM
- **Arbitration player non-tendered by analytics GM same season as loyalty discount eligible:** non-tender takes precedence — player cannot receive loyalty discount if non-tendered
