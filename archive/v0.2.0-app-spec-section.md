# v0.2.0-app-spec-section.md
# Feature: Live Org, Market Dynamics, In-Season Firings, News Feed

---

## Scope

v0.2.0 is a full release. It makes the minor league system live, adds market-driven GM behavior, in-season firing logic, a news feed, and restructures all LLM usage to narrative/flavor only. All game decisions are now procedural.

---

## LLM Restructure (Breaking Change from v0.1.0)

### Decision calls removed
The following LLM call points are removed and replaced with procedural logic:
- Draft picks — decision is now purely position-adjusted value + GM personality parameters. LLM is called only for flavor text on a sampled ~20 picks per draft (not all 600).
- Trade proposals — fully procedural. GM philosophy + standings + asset value formula drives all trade logic.
- Free agent bids — fully procedural. Need × budget × market formula.
- Front office changes — fully procedural. Threshold math with personality modifiers.

### LLM call points retained (narrative only)
- **Season narrative** — 1 call per season. 2-3 paragraph recap for Timeline tab. Unchanged.
- **Transaction flavor** — one-liner per transaction, batched 10 per call. Unchanged.
- **News feed headlines** — one-liner per news event (firing, call-up, waiver claim, trade, injury). Batched 10 per call.
- **Draft pick flavor** — reasoning string for ~20 sampled picks per draft shown in draft room UI. Not all picks — just enough to make the room feel alive.

### LLM is a narrator, not a decision maker
All prompts going forward describe what happened and ask for a vivid one-liner or narrative paragraph. No prompts ask the LLM to choose between options or return structured decision JSON.

**Bug:** `owner_died` event only updates `owner_name`, never touches `owner_personality`.
**Fix:** Both `owner_died` and `owner_sold_team` events must update both `owner_name` AND `owner_personality` with a freshly randomized value. The heir or new owner gets a completely fresh personality — they are not their parent.

## Live Minor League System

### Player Levels
Every player has a level: MLB / AAA / AA / A / Rookie. Level determines which roster they appear on and whether they're eligible for call-up.

### Spring Training Cuts (start of each season)
- Each team trims from 40-man to 25-man active roster
- Cut criteria (procedural, GM philosophy drives weighting):
  - Overall rating < 45 with no upside (potential D): release
  - Blocked by better player at same position with higher ceiling: send down
  - Analytics GM: cuts based on projected WAR, not reputation
  - Old-school GM: favors veterans, slower to cut experienced players
- Released players go directly to free agent pool (no waivers for spring cuts)

### DFA (Designated for Assignment)
- When a team needs 40-man space mid-season: DFA a player
- DFA'd player has a 3-game sim window on waivers before becoming a free agent
- Team can also outright the player to minors if unclaimed (uses an option)

### Waiver Wire
- All DFA'd and released players pass through waivers
- Claim order: reverse current standings order
- Claim window: 3 games of sim time
- If claimed: claiming team gets player, original team gets nothing
- If unclaimed: player becomes a free agent
- Small market / analytics GMs actively monitor waivers — higher claim rate
- Contending teams claim for depth; rebuilding teams claim for upside

### Call-Ups
Triggers (evaluated every 5 games):
- MLB roster drops below 23 active (injuries)
- A starter has OPS < .580 over last 15 AB and AAA replacement is rated higher
- Starting pitcher ERA > 7.00 over last 3 starts and AAA arm available
- Prospect in AAA has overall rating >= MLB starter at same position + 5 points

Call-up logic:
- Pull best available at needed position from AAA first, then AA
- If no minor leaguer qualifies: check waiver wire, then free agents
- Service time clock starts on first call-up date

### Send-Downs
Triggers:
- Roster needs a specific position after injury and no 40-man space
- Player OPS < .560 over 20+ AB with better option in AAA
- Pitcher ERA > 6.50 over 4+ starts with AAA replacement available
- Player must have option remaining — if no options left, must DFA instead

### Service Time
- Tracks games on MLB roster per player
- 172 games = 1 full year of service time
- 6 years service time = free agency eligibility
- Teams may manipulate service time by keeping top prospects in AAA ~15 extra games to delay free agency by 1 year
- Analytics GMs more likely to manipulate; old-school GMs less likely

### Prospect Development (runs every 10 games, not just offseason)
- Players 18-25 in AA/A/Rookie: ratings tick up 0-1 points based on coachability × work_ethic × coaching staff quality
- AAA players (any age): stable, preparing for MLB
- Hot streaks (5+ game hit streak in minors): +1 temporary visibility boost, increases call-up priority
- Busts: if a player reaches age 26 still in AA with potential C/D, potential is permanently downgraded to D, overall capped at 65

## Market Dynamics & GM Archetypes

### GM Archetypes (expanded from v0.1.0 philosophy field)

Each GM philosophy now maps to a concrete behavioral archetype that drives all procedural decisions:

**Analytics (small/medium market bias):**
- Aggressively targets waiver wire for undervalued players
- Trades veterans 6-10 games before deadline (early, maximizes return)
- Non-tenders players entering arbitration year 3+ if projected salary > value
- Draft: heavily weights upside (potential A/B) over current rating
- Never pays market rate for free agents — always seeks discount

**Old-School (any market):**
- Waiver wire: claims veterans, ignores prospects
- Trades veterans only at deadline, not before
- Loyal to veterans — slower to DFA, slower to call up prospects
- Draft: weights current rating over potential
- Pays premium for proven players in free agency

**Balanced:**
- Middle ground on all decisions
- Deadline trades happen at game 33-37 window
- Mix of veteran loyalty and prospect development

### Small Market Survival Behaviors
Small and medium market teams procedurally:
- Set a veteran trade threshold: any player age 30+ with 2 or fewer contract years becomes trade bait if team is 8+ games out of playoff position by game 30
- Actively claim waiver wire players rated 50-65 (the sweet spot — good enough to help, cheap enough to keep)
- Non-tender players entering arbitration if salary projection exceeds $8M
- Never pursue top free agents (budget hard cap enforced in GM logic)
- Accept prospect-heavy returns in trades even when offering lesser packages

### Large/Mega Market Behaviors
- Buy at deadline: actively pursue rentals, willing to trade prospects
- Pursue top free agents, willing to exceed luxury tax
- Lower tolerance for rebuilding — owner patience erodes faster
- Claim waiver wire for depth only, not upside

### Trade Deadline Logic (procedural, game 30-40 window)
For each team, evaluate trade posture at game 30:
```
if games_back <= 5: BUYER — acquire veterans, trade prospects
if games_back >= 10: SELLER — trade veterans, acquire prospects
if 5 < games_back < 10: NEUTRAL — small moves only

buyer_aggression = owner_patience_inverse × standings_urgency
seller_price = prospect_value × market_size_modifier
```

Trade matching:
1. Identify buyer teams and seller teams
2. For each seller's available veterans: find best matching buyer by positional need
3. Generate trade package: prospect tier matched to veteran value
4. Both GMs evaluate via personality parameters — analytics GM demands more prospects, old-school GM demands proven players back
5. If no match found after 3 attempts: no trade

## In-Season Firing Logic

### Firing Threshold Formula
```
firing_threshold = base_games_under_500
  × owner_patience_modifier
  × gm_aggression_modifier (for manager firings only)

owner_patience_modifier:
  meddling:  0.6x  (fires earliest)
  win-now:   0.8x
  patient:   1.5x
  hands-off: 2.0x  (fires latest)

gm_aggression_modifier (manager leash only):
  aggressive:   0.75x  (shorter leash)
  moderate:     1.0x
  conservative: 1.2x   (longer leash)

base_games_under_500: 8 games (i.e. 8 more losses than wins)
```

### Firing Chain
**Owner fires GM:**
- Evaluated every 10 games
- Trigger: team record hits firing_threshold (owner_patience_modifier only, no gm_aggression_modifier)
- Interim GM: conservative philosophy, moderate aggression by default, tenure = 0
- New permanent GM hired in offseason with randomized personality

**GM fires manager:**
- Evaluated every 5 games
- Trigger: team record hits firing_threshold (both modifiers applied)
- Only fires if owner personality is NOT meddling (meddling owner handles this directly)
- Interim manager: promoted from bench coach, manager_style inherited, job_security = 5

**Meddling owner fires manager directly:**
- Bypasses GM entirely
- Lower threshold than GM would use
- GM is notified (narrative event generated) but has no veto

**Owner fires manager directly (non-meddling):**
- Only when record is catastrophic: base threshold × 2.5x (very high bar)
- Generates "owner breaks glass" news event
- GM is embarrassed — GM job_security -= 2

### Interim Replacements
- Interim manager: bench coach steps up, degraded ratings (manager_style same, all ratings -10)
- Interim GM: most senior scout becomes interim, conservative/moderate defaults
- Interim roles last until end of season — permanent hire happens in offseason
- Interims cannot be fired mid-season (stability floor)

## News Feed

### Live Ticker
- Scrolling bar displayed at top of all tabs during active sim
- Shows last 5 events, scrolls as new ones arrive
- Each item: timestamp + one-liner (LLM-generated)
- Events that generate ticker items: game results (score only, no LLM), firings, call-ups, DFA, waiver claims, trades, injuries, milestones

### News Tab (new tab in UI)
- Full chronological feed, most recent at top
- Each item: game number, date, event type badge, headline (LLM one-liner)
- Filterable by: All / My Team (if team selected) / Transactions / Front Office / Injuries / Milestones
- Click any item: expands to full detail (player card, team context, transaction details)
- LLM one-liners generated in batches of 10, queued and filled as sim runs

### Event Types That Generate News Items
| Event | Badge | LLM? |
|---|---|---|
| Game result | GAME | No — just score |
| Call-up | ROSTER | Yes |
| Send-down | ROSTER | Yes |
| DFA | ROSTER | Yes |
| Waiver claim | TRANSACTION | Yes |
| Trade | TRANSACTION | Yes |
| Free agent signing | TRANSACTION | Yes |
| Release | TRANSACTION | Yes |
| Manager fired | FRONT OFFICE | Yes |
| GM fired | FRONT OFFICE | Yes |
| Manager resigned | FRONT OFFICE | Yes |
| Owner sold team | FRONT OFFICE | Yes |
| Owner died | FRONT OFFICE | Yes |
| Player injury | INJURY | Yes |
| Player milestone | MILESTONE | Yes |

## Known Edge Cases

- **DFA'd player claimed before window expires:** remove from waiver wire immediately, log transaction, generate news item
- **No team claims a player:** after 3-game window, player becomes free agent silently (no news item unless notable — overall 65+)
- **Interim GM at trade deadline:** interim GMs do NOT make trades — too conservative. Skip trade evaluation for teams with interim GM.
- **Call-up when no AAA players available:** check AA, then A, then waiver wire, then free agents. If truly none available, leave roster short temporarily.
- **Player with no options remaining gets send-down trigger:** must DFA instead. Generate news item.
- **Both owner and GM want to fire manager simultaneously:** owner's decision takes precedence. One event, not two.
- **Firing during turbo sim:** all firings still fire correctly in turbo — do not skip firing checks during burst sim.
- **Service time manipulation:** only analytics GMs do this, and only for players with potential A or B. Never manipulate service time for potential C/D.
